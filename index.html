Project Goals¶

The team, Yali Tiomkin and Rafi Deykin, will be exploring 2 datasets which explore health insurance and disabilities information in Louisiana, by parish. The goal of this project is to gain an understanding of how several varibles related to health and levels of insurance, as well as income are related to each other. Ultimately, we are interested in understanding the effects that Medicaid expansion had on the rates of insurance in the state of Louisiana. Did Medicaid expansion lead to higher rates of insurance? Did it have different effects by parish? Was one sex more greatly impacted than another? These are the types of questions that we are ultimately interested in answering

Health Insurance Data - https://data.census.gov/cedsci/table?q=S2701%3A%20SELECTED%20CHARACTERISTICS%20OF%20HEALTH%20INSURANCE%20COVERAGE%20IN%20THE%20UNITED%20STATES&g=0100000US_0400000US22%240500000&tid=ACSST1Y2021.S2701

A quick note on Unicode Characters¶

There is an interesting error that we get when we try to access the column "Male". This is because there are indentations (more specifically non breaking spaces) in the original dataset that have been handled automatically by PANDAS replacing them with the "\xa0". Lets look at an example

Model Questions¶

We have seen that there are differences between the pre Medicad and post Medicaid expansion datasets. Particularly, we have seen that insurance and uninsurance rates vary the most by age from pre Medicaid expansion to post Medicaid expansion.

Instead of making a bunch of graphs and getting the most important features that way, we could use SKLearn, a machine learning library with various models, to help us understand which variables are most important for predicting rates of insurance. To do this, we need to fit two separate models (most likely linear regressors as we are predicting values) to each of the data sets. We can then use built in SKlearn fuctions to determine which features are important to the prediction of insurance rates. Doing this will allow us to see if there are changes between the two datasets in terms of feature importance. This will allow us to understand what type of effect Medicad expansion had on Louisiana.

The second question that we want to answer using our models is if we are able to predict the rates of insurance of one dataset using the features of the other dataset.
